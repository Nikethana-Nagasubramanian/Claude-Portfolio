Problem Statement

Once a site was approved, lease management fell apart into disconnected local files, generic PDF tools, and manual re-reading of 80+ page documents.

What I Did

I designed a lease management dashboard and Clara, an NLP assistant that turns static lease documents into answerable, trustworthy data.

Fig: Clara answering a portfolio-wide lease question from the dashboard

Problem

The burden of disconnected information.

Users evaluated sites inside our platform, then dropped into a completely disconnected, manual process to manage the resulting lease. Signed leases lived as local files, searched with generic PDF tools that could return keyword frequency but nothing close to an intelligent summary.

The scale made this unsustainable: portfolios with 160+ documents, and leases running 80+ pages where a single question meant re-reading dozens of pages to find the answer.

Solution 1: Dashboard

A quick-reference layer over complex lease data.

I designed a dashboard that transforms dense lease data into scannable summaries, reducing cognitive load by surfacing what's urgent first. Highlighted cards carry the key data points for fast decisions, while a detailed table view stays available for anyone who needs to go deeper.

Solution 2: Clara

An assistant built for retrieval inside long documents, not just chat.

Clara operates in two states depending on where you are: from the lease management homepage, it answers questions across the entire portfolio; inside an individual lease page, it answers questions scoped to that document alone.

Process

Beyond "just another chat interface."

Clara's job is to give people their time back, but accuracy alone wasn't enough to earn that. Every answer links back to the source document, so users can verify the answer instead of taking it on faith — that's what turns a chat box into something people actually trust.

I iterated through multiple versions before landing on v2, which made snapshot elements more prominent and clickable, and worked through how Clara should handle edge cases: complex multi-part queries and outright system errors, not just the happy path.

Reflection

Impactful products solve problems while giving users control.

The team designed and validated Clara's core functionality and states, and handed off a complete, engineering-ready flow. Shipping this from 0 to 1 — problem framing through validated design — is one of the projects I'm proudest of.

Product Roadmap

Clara was designed as infrastructure, not a feature.

Near-term, the plan is to monitor adoption and gather feedback, then build toward automatic contract renewal alerts and knowledge-base integration. Longer-term, Clara is meant to grow from a chat interface into an indispensable part of the workflow — one that can proactively surface insights and alerts instead of only answering when asked.
